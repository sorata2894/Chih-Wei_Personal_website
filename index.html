Chih‑Wei Tseng

Research Assistant • Institute of Electrical and Control Engineering • @NYCU

I am a Research Assistant at National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University (NYCU), supervised by Chair Professor Bing-Fei Wu. My research focuses on generative, efficient, and explainable AI for remote physiological sensing and intelligent robotic systems (including autonomous driving).

Recent work includes generative ECG-from-PPG modeling for multi-class arrhythmia detection; interpretable, memory-augmented trajectory prediction; and an industry collaboration on interpretability for imaging-to-rPPG. I also lead several master’s students on generative AI for autonomous-driving scenarios and rare-event data. During my M.S., I developed a contact-free rPPG atrial-fibrillation detection system that runs in real time on mobile devices and achieves >90% accuracy under motion and lighting variations; this work was featured as a Feature Article in IEEE JBHI.

Bio

Chih-Wei Tseng is a Research Assistant at NYCU, advised by Chair Professor Bing-Fei Wu. His work centers on generative, efficient, and explainable AI for remote physiological sensing and robotics.

During his M.S. at NYCU (advisor: Chair Professor Bing-Fei Wu), he developed a lightweight, real-time rPPG system for atrial fibrillation detection. The study used 7,216 facial-video clips from 452 subjects, achieved mobile deployment with over 90% accuracy under motion and lighting variations, and was published as a Feature Article in IEEE JBHI, with ~99% reduction in size/parameters/FLOPs and ~50% latency reduction compared to prior work.

As an undergraduate at Tamkang University (advisor: Distinguished Professor Ching-Chang Wong), he built a gesture-based robotic-arm poker system combining a YOLOv4 card recognizer (98% accuracy) with multi-agent reinforcement learning (~70% win rate). He now leads three master’s students on LLMs and generative AI, works on memory-based trajectory prediction, and collaborates with FaceHeart Inc. on interpretability for imaging-to-rPPG.
