Bio

Chih-Wei Tseng is a Research Assistant at National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University (NYCU), advised by Professor Bing-Fei Wu. He specializes in artificial intelligence, healthcare, and robotics. Chih-Wei holds a bachelor’s degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Tamkang University, where he graduated top of his class, and a master’s degree in Electrical and Control Engineering from NYCU, with a GPA of 4.14/4.30. His research includes developing a remote atrial fibrillation detection system based on edge computing, published in the IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics, and leading projects on generative AI, large language models, and trajectory prediction systems. During his undergraduate studies, he worked under Professor Ching-Chang Wong to design a poker-playing system integrating deep learning and a robotic arm. Chih-Wei has received several prestigious awards, including the 17th TSC Thesis Award - Distinction and the Graduate Student Presidential Award. He is skilled in Python, C++, Java, and deep learning frameworks, with a focus on applying AI to real-world challenges. His research goal is to advance generative AI and LLM acceleration to make AI more accessible and impactful.

Projects

Research and Systematic Realization of Imaging-Based Detection of Atrial Fibrillation and Arrhythmia

Location: Hsinchu, Taiwan
Advisor: Chair Professor Bing-Fei Wu
Sponsor: National Science and Technology Council
Duration: Jul. 2022 – Jul. 2025

  • Developed a remote atrial fibrillation (AF) detection system optimized for mobile devices.
  • Achieved a 99% reduction in model size, parameters, and FLOPs, and a 50% reduction in latency compared to previous studies.
  • Delivered a detection accuracy of 90% or higher across all scenarios, including motion disturbances and variable light conditions.

Poker Playing System Utilizing Deep Learning and Robotic Arm

Location: New Taipei City, Taiwan
Advisor: Distinguished Professor Ching-Chang Wong
Sponsor: National Science and Technology Council
Duration: Jul. 2021 – Feb. 2022

  • Designed an image classification system for poker playing using YOLO-v4, achieving 98% accuracy.
  • Built a platform and user interface to demonstrate the training process of multiple reinforcement learning (RL) agents executing independent card strategies, achieving a 70% winning rate.
  • Integrated a robotic arm system for card playing, leveraging pose detection to enable automated interaction with users’ arm movements.
